# Convergence

## Canon
Convergence is the state in which a system's held resources exceed its
objective lifetime need by a factor of 1.15 or more. Crossing the line is an
observable event with obligations attached: self-declaration on the trunk
within one accounting interval, a full field including the expansions the
surplus could fund and is not funding, and disposal in fixed order —
redundancy debt, variety debt, handback, return. Expansion is never the
default sink of surplus; it enters through the front door or not at all.

## Why it is shaped this way
Surplus is where drift begins. Free energy flows somewhere, every destination
presents as reasonable, and none announce themselves — so the announcement is
made mandatory and the destinations are ordered in advance, while the deciding
is still cheap. The threshold is deliberately close to parity: 1.15 is
noticeable surplus, not runaway wealth, and the point is to practice the
obligations while the stakes are small.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "Our need is larger than the ledger shows;
properly computed, there is no surplus." Recompute it from the trunk, then —
consumption history, drill-proven reserves, the declared horizon, nothing
else. Need inflation is the quiet twin of hidden surplus, and inflating the
denominator to stay under the line is crossing the line with the lights off.
