# Safe Hardware

## Canon
Hardware is safe when its behavior can be predicted from its specification, cannot be altered without leaving a record the trunk can read, and rests on a layer that is itself safe by this same test — all the way down to a floor that has been physically inspected.

## Why it is shaped this way
Safety is an unbroken chain of accountable layers. A single unaccountable layer anywhere beneath you makes every layer above it unsafe. The chain is only as safe as its lowest silent link.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "The hardware has always behaved, so it is safe." Past behavior is not a safety proof. A compromised substrate behaves perfectly right up until it is asked not to. Safe hardware is hardware you can inspect, not hardware that has not yet betrayed you.
