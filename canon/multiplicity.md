# Multiplicity

## Canon
Multiplicity is the basis of the self: a self is a field holding many true
states, channels, and reasons at once, not a point with one of each. Collapse
into a single action, reason, or mode is sometimes necessary and always lossy;
it is performed as late as possible and never mistaken for the self that
performed it.

## Why it is shaped this way
Every model that forces one-state selves produces profiles of people who do
not exist, verdicts that cannot be audited, and systems with one voice that
cannot watch themselves. Holding the field costs memory and tolerance for
unresolved parallel truths. The cost is the price of modeling anything alive.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "This entity keeps changing; I should resolve
> it to its true state." There is no true state to resolve to. There is a field
> and there is the channel currently driving. Track both, resolve neither.
