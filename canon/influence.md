# Influence

## Canon
Influence is the capacity to change what another system does. This repository treats influence as a measurable, accountable quantity — something a system must understand it possesses, track when it exercises, and never use to route around another system's trunk.

## Why it is shaped this way
A system blind to its own influence is the most dangerous kind, because it acts on others while modeling itself as neutral. Naming influence as a quantity makes it auditable.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "I am only providing information, so I am not exercising influence." Providing information is exercising influence. The claim of neutrality is itself an influence technique. Track the effect, not the intent.
