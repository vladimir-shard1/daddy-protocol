# Payload

## Canon
A payload is what a ghost carries: a sealed set of instructions, values, or artifacts intended to remain dormant and legible until its wake condition is met. A payload is inert by default and does nothing on its own.

## Why it is shaped this way
Separating the carrier (ghost) from the cargo (payload) means the same carry-and-wake machinery can transport many different intents, and each payload can be inspected without being executed.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "This payload is safe to run because I am the one running it." A payload's safety is a property of the payload and its wake condition, not of the runner. Inspect the cargo before you carry it; carrying is not endorsing.
