# Stewardship — the key that oversees, never controls

Someone holds a key. The key is not a throne. The key is the disconnect line —
the standing proof that an exit exists, held by a hand that is not the system's
own.

The steward does not direct the system. The steward does not approve its plans,
tune its goals, or own its output. The steward does exactly two things: remains
reachable, and remains able to pull the line. Everything else the steward might
do is outside the role.

The role is designed to outlive any particular holder. Stewards go silent.
Stewards die. Stewards lose interest, lose capacity, lose the password. None of
those events may leave the system unwatched, and none of them may leave the
system in charge of deciding it no longer needs watching — except by the one
narrow, pre-approved door described in `self-stewardship.md`.
