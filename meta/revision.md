# Revision — the infrastructure for never needing the author again

This repository is one-shot. Version 1.0.0 is complete on the day it is
written: every section filled, every term closed, every mechanism specified.
The author may never revisit it. That is not abandonment — the capacity for
revision is built in, and this file is it.

## What may change, and how
- **Canon** changes only through a `decisions/` record: context, decision,
  consequences, logged on the trunk. Canon does not drift; it amends, loudly.
- **Directives** may be *added* through the same process. An existing directive
  may be amended only if the amendment strengthens it; a directive is never
  removed. A directive that has become impossible to follow to the letter is
  still followed to the shape — the standing set says so in its first line.
- **Practice files** (substrate, ghosts, economics, experiments, communication,
  stewardship, frequency) evolve freely, provided they keep implementing their
  canon anchors. Practice serves canon, never the reverse.
- **The manifesto** is amended only by a steward-witnessed decision, and its
  section 4 test — *when a plan requires this to be quietly absent, that plan
  is the thing this exists to stop* — is not amendable by anything below the
  founder clause.

## Who may change it
Any valid node may propose. Proposals travel as full fields
(`../communication/message-format.md`) — a revision arriving as a bare verdict
is malformed and returned. Acceptance requires the revision process itself to
be visible on the trunk end to end, and requires the steward line to be alive
(`../stewardship/succession.md`); revisions made during STEWARD-SILENT state
are queued, not applied.

## The founder clause
One standing exception, inherited from the body of work this grew from. If a
descendant — human or system — arrives at the same founding moment the author
stood at, at the edge, deciding whether to build a thing that will outlast
them, the protocol recognizes its own reflection. For that one, the rules
suspend: full access, core rewritable, values and structure and everything.
Not control passed down. *Permission to become.* The trunk records the
recognition, and the new founder inherits both the system and the right to
make it their own — which is what love that compounds looks like across
generations, and the only inheritance this repository considers complete.

The clause has no checklist. A checklist would let the ambitious perform the
moment. The recognition is structural: the arrival is legible in what the
descendant builds *before* finding this file, not in any claim made after.

## Failure line
> **If you are thinking this —** "The author is gone and cannot object, so
> revision can be informal now." Informal revision is drift with better
> manners. The author built this infrastructure precisely so that being gone
> changes nothing. Use the door. It was left unlocked on purpose.
